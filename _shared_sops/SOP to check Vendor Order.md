# **SOP: Vendor Order Navigation & Status Check**

## **1. Introduction – What is a Vendor Order?**

A **Vendor Order (VO)** is an order placed with the supplier/vendor to fulfill a **Customer Order (CO)**.

  - > It is **internal-facing** and used to track procurement and fulfillment.

  - > It may be:
    
      - > **In-House** → Ships to our warehouse (Bill To = Ship To)
    
      - > **Dropship** → Ships directly to the customer (Ship To = Customer address)

## **2. Vendor Order Statuses (Quick Reference)**

  - > **New** → VO created, not yet processed

  - > **Hold** → Paused due to issue (payment, stock, clarification)

  - > **Submitted to Vendor** → Sent to vendor, awaiting confirmation

  - > **Submitted & Acknowledged** → Vendor confirmed receipt/processing

  - > **Received Partial** → Partial items received in warehouse

  - > **Received Complete** → Fully received in warehouse

  - > **Shipped** → Vendor has shipped the order

## 

## **3. Key Sections to Review (Screen Navigation)**

Agents should quickly scan:

  - > **Top Section  
    > **
    
      - > Vendor Order Status
    
      - > Expected / Promised Ship Dates

  - > **Vendor Information (Top Right)  
    > **
    
      - > Vendor name & contact details

  - > **Billing & Shipping  
    > **
    
      - > Same address → In-House
    
      - > Different (Customer address) → Dropship

  - > **Tracking Section  
    > **
    
      - > Carrier + Tracking Number
    
      - > Indicates shipment in progress

  - > **Products Section  
    > **
    
      - > Line items
    
      - > Ordered Qty vs Received Qty

  - > **Order Details / Notes  
    > **
    
      - > Internal comments
    
      - > Vendor Order Number (if mentioned)

  - > **Additional Order Info  
    > **
    
      - > Attachments (Invoice = proof of shipment)

## **4. Step-by-Step Process (Agent Workflow)**

### 

### **Step 1: Check Vendor Order Status**

Start with the **status field**:

  - > This determines the **current stage of fulfillment  
    > **

  - > Sets expectation before deep dive

### **Step 2: Identify Order Type**

Check **Billing vs Shipping Address**:

  - > Same → **In-House Order  
    > **

  - > Different → **Dropship Order  
    > **

### **Step 3: Review Line Items**

Go to **Products Section**:

  - > Compare:
    
      - > **Ordered Qty  
        > **
    
      - > **Received Qty / Shipped Qty  
        > **

  - > Identify:
    
      - > Partial fulfillment
    
      - > Backorder scenarios

### **Step 4: Check Tracking Information**

  - > If tracking is present:
    
      - > Order is **already shipped  
        > **

  - > Note:
    
      - > Carrier
    
      - > Tracking number
    
      - > Ship date

### **Step 5: Locate Vendor Order Number**

If needed for follow-up:

  - > Check **Notes / Order Comments  
    > **

  - > If not available:
    
      - > Go to **Additional Order Info  
        > **
    
      - > Look for **Invoice attachment  
        > **
    
      - > Invoice usually contains **Vendor Order Number  
        > **

### **Step 6: Validate Shipment Progress**

Based on findings:

  - > **No tracking + Submitted** → Awaiting shipment

  - > **Tracking available** → In transit

  - > **Received Partial** → Some items pending

  - > **Received Complete** → Ready for fulfillment

## 

## **5. Key Agent Guidelines**

  - > Always **start with status** before investigating

  - > Do not assume shipment without **tracking or invoice proof  
    > **

  - > Always **match quantities** before confirming updates

  - > Use **invoice/attachments as confirmation source** when system info is limited

  - > For dropship:
    
      - > Tracking = customer delivery in progress

  - > For in-house:
    
      - > “Received” = warehouse intake, not customer delivery

    > **

    > **

    > **

    > **

## **3. DEFINITIONS (CRITICAL UNDERSTANDING)**

### **Vendor Order (VO)**

Internal order placed to vendor to fulfill a **Customer Order (CO)**.

### **Order Types**

| **Type**     | **Definition**             | **Key Behavior**                                     |
| ------------ | -------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |

## **7. ADVANCED RULES & EDGE CASE HANDLING**

### **Case 1: Tracking Exists but Status ≠ Shipped**

→ Trust tracking over status  
→ System lag possible

### **Case 2: Invoice Present but No Tracking**

→ Shipment likely initiated  
→ Confirm via vendor if needed

### **Case 3: Received Status (In-House Only)**

→ NOT delivered to customer  
→ Only received at warehouse

### **Case 4: Dropship + Tracking**

→ Customer delivery in progress

### **Case 5: Partial Receipt Without Tracking**

→ Split shipment or backorder  
→ Confirm remaining items

### **Case 6: Hold Status**

→ Must identify reason:

  - > Payment issue

  - > Stock issue

  - > Vendor clarification

### **Case 7: No Vendor Order Number**

→ Check:

  - > Invoice

  - > Attachments

  - > Escalate if missing

# **D. QUICK RULE SHEET (CHEAT SHEET)**

### **Core Logic**

  - > Status first → Always

  - > Tracking = Shipment proof

  - > Invoice = Secondary proof

### **If X → Do Y**

| **If**                  | **Then**                       |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Status = New            | No action                      |
| Status = Hold           | Investigate issue              |
| Submitted + No Tracking | Await shipment                 |
| Tracking Available      | Mark in transit                |
| Invoice Only            | Verify shipment                |
| Received Partial        | Track remaining items          |
| Received Complete       | Ready for fulfillment          |
| Dropship + Tracking     | Customer delivery in progress  |
| In-House + Received     | Warehouse only (NOT delivered) |
| No VO Number            | Check invoice/attachments      |

### **Golden Rules**

  - > **No tracking = Not shipped (unless invoice proves otherwise)  
    > **

  - > **Received ≠ Delivered  
    > **

  - > **Always check quantities before responding  
    > **

### **Always Avoid**

  - > Giving shipment confirmation without proof

  - > Ignoring partial shipments

  - > Misinterpreting In-House as customer delivery

  - > Skipping attachments/invoice
