# **<span class="underline">SOP: Post Order Status Check</span>** 

## **PURPOSE**

To standardize how agents check order status, validate ETA, and communicate accurate updates to customers.

## **SCOPE**

Applies to all Sales Account Managers handling order status inquiries and follow-ups.

# **ORDER TYPES – BASIC UNDERSTANDING (VERY IMPORTANT)**

Before checking any order status, agents must understand that **all orders fall into two primary fulfillment types**:

**IN-HOUSE ORDERS (WAREHOUSE FLOW)**

**  
** These are orders where the product is first shipped **from the vendor to our warehouse**, and then shipped **from our warehouse to the customer**.

### **How It Works:**

1.  > Vendor ships item → to **our warehouse  
    > **

2.  > Warehouse receives & processes item

3.  > Order is shipped → to **customer  
    > **

### **Key Things to Remember:**

  - > ETA in Backoffice = **Arrival to warehouse (NOT customer delivery)  
    > **

  - > There is an **extra step (warehouse processing)  
    > **

  - > **Delays can happen:  
    > **
    
      - > **From vendor → warehouse  
        > **
    
      - > **Or inside warehouse processing**

### **Common Statuses:**

  - > Coming In House

  - > Coming In House w/ BO

  - > Partial – Coming In House

  - > Dropship / In House (Split orders)

### **Agent Focus:**

**Always check:**

  - > **ETA in Backoffice  
    > **

  - > **Vendor shipment status (if ETA unclear)**

**DROPSHIP ORDERS (VENDOR FLOW)**

**  
** These are orders where the product is shipped **directly from the vendor to the customer**, without coming to our warehouse.

### **How It Works:**

1.  > Order is placed with the vendor on their website or with a quote.

2.  > Vendor processes order

3.  > Vendor ships directly → to **customer  
    > **

### **Key Things to Remember:**

  - > No warehouse involvement**  
    > **

  - > **Delays can happen:  
    > **
    
      - > **From vendor → warehouse  
        > **

  - > **Or inside warehouse processing**

  - > **Vendor controls:  
    > **
    
      - > **Processing  
        > **
    
      - > **Shipping  
        > **
    
      - > **Timeline**

### 

### **Common Statuses:**

  - > Placed for Dropship

  - > Dropship w/ BO

  - > Dropship Shipped

  - > Partial – Dropship

  - > Dropship / In House (Split orders)

### **Agent Focus:**

Always check:

  - > Vendor order details

  - > Shipment status

  - > Tracking (once shipped)

**CORE PRINCIPLE (VERY IMPORTANT)**

Do NOT treat statuses in isolation. For EVERY order:

1.  > **Identify what the status actually means (In-house / Dropship / Shipped)  
    > **

2.  > **Compare Ordered vs Shipped (always check partials)  
    > **

3.  > **Check Backoffice First for ETA and updates  
    > **

4.  > **Validate using Vendor /Carrier  
    > **

5.  > **Only then communicate to the customer  
    > **

\*\*\* **Golden Rule**: **BO → Validate → Then Speak**

# **STATUS-WISE HANDLING** 

## **1. Coming In House**

  - > **Coming In house status :** Inventory is expected to arrive at the warehouse in an expected time frame.

### 

### **When is this status used?**

This status is used when the item is **available with the vendor** and has been ordered to be shipped to **our warehouse first**, before being delivered to the customer.  
It indicates that the inventory is **in transit to the warehouse** and not yet ready for final shipment.

**Action to be taken**

  - > Start with **Backoffice ETA check**

  - > Validate if shipment is actually sent by vendor (invoice / order status)

  - > Coordinate with **warehouse if** **ETA is near or passed if needed**

  - > If unclear → **follow up with vendor for shipping confirmation**

  - > **Final step**: Inform customer with **ETA to warehouse + delivery buffer (1 week)**

## **2. Coming In House w/ BO**

  - > **Coming In house w/ BO :** Partial Inventory is expected to arrive at the warehouse in an expected time frame and the reminder is out of stock but expected to be restocked soon.

### 

### **When is this status used?**

This status is used when the order is **partially available**, and the remaining items are **on backorder (out of stock with the vendor)**.  
The available items will arrive at the warehouse soon, while the rest will ship **once the vendor restocks them**.

### 

**Action to be taken**

  - > Check **available vs backordered items  
    > **

  - > Review both **ETA (available items)** and **BO ETA  
    > **

  - > Validate BO timeline with vendor (priority) if needed

  - > Evaluate **split shipment option (if needed)  
    > **

  - > Inform customer clearly about **partial availability + remaining timeline**

This status is used when the order is **split into two parts**:

  - > One part is **dropshipped directly to the customer  
    > **

  - > Break order into **two parts (DO NOT treat as one)  
    > **

  - > For In-House → track shipment to warehouse or get an ETA

  - > Validate both sides separately (vendor + warehouse)

  - > Communicate clearly to customer about **split shipments and timelines**

## **A. Detailed Written SOP**

### **1. Purpose**

To standardize how agents check order status, validate ETA, and communicate accurate updates to customers.

### **2. Scope**

Applies to all Sales Account Managers handling order status inquiries, follow-ups, vendor checks, warehouse checks, and customer updates after order placement.

## **4. Order Types – Basic Understanding**

Either the orders will come in house to our warehouse or will be shipped to customers directly.

### **4.1 In-House Orders (Warehouse Flow)**

These are orders where the product is first shipped from the vendor to our warehouse, then from our warehouse to the customer.

**Flow:  
** Vendor → Our Warehouse → Customer

**Key Rules**

  - > ETA in Backoffice means **arrival to our warehouse**, not delivery to customers

  - > There is an extra warehouse processing step before final shipment.

  - > Delays can happen:
    
      - > Vendor to warehouse
    
      - > Inside warehouse processing

**Common statuses**

  - > Coming In House

  - > Coming In House w/ BO

  - > Partial – Coming In House

  - > Dropship / In House

  - > Dropship / In House w/ BO

  - > Shipped Orders – Partial – Coming In House

**Agent focus**

  - > Check ETA in Backoffice

  - > Check vendor shipment status if ETA is missing, near, or passed

  - > Add delivery buffer after warehouse receipt when communicating to customer

### **4.2 Dropship Orders (Vendor Flow)**

These are orders where the vendor ships directly to the customer.

**Flow:  
** Vendor → Customer

**Key Rules**

  - > No warehouse involvement

  - > Vendor controls processing, shipping, and timeline

  - > Tracking is critical once shipped

**Common statuses**

  - > Placed for Dropship

  - > Dropship w/ BO

  - > Dropship Shipped

  - > Partial – Dropship

  - > Dropship / In House

  - > Shipped Orders – Partial – Dropshipped

**Agent focus**

  - > Check vendor order details

  - > Check shipment status

  - > Check tracking once shipped

## **5. Standard Status Check Method – Universal Workflow**

### **Phase 1: Backoffice Check**

For every order:

1.  > Open the order in Backoffice.

2.  > Verify the order status at the top.

3.  > Scroll to the Product Section.

4.  > Check:
    
      - > ETA
    
      - > Qty Ordered
    
      - > Qty Shipped
    
      - > Tracking section
    
      - > Line-level fulfillment details

5.  > Determine whether the order is:
    
      - > Fully pending
    
      - > Partially shipped
    
      - > Fully shipped
    
      - > In-house
    
      - > Dropship
    
      - > Split
    
      - > Backordered

**Always avoid:**

  - > Speaking to the customer before checking Qty Ordered vs Qty Shipped.

  - > Giving a delivery promise based only on status name.

### **Phase 2: ETA Validation**

After Backoffice review:

  - > If ETA is available and still valid, use it as the working reference.

  - > If ETA is near or passed:
    
      - > Check warehouse if applicable
    
      - > Check vendor order
    
      - > Check tracking if applicable

  - > If ETA is missing:
    
      - > Move immediately to vendor order validation

**Important**

  - > In-house ETA = ETA to our warehouse

  - > Dropship ETA = ETA toward customer, if provided

  - > For in-house shipments, add a **1-week delivery buffer** after warehouse receipt unless a more accurate confirmed internal timeline exists

### **Phase 3: Vendor Order Check (Critical)**

When ETA is missing, unclear, delayed, or tracking is missing, follow this exact process:

1.  > Open Vendor Order.

2.  > Go to **Additional Information and Notes**.

3.  > Look for:
    
      - > Vendor Order Number in Notes
    
      - > Vendor Invoice in Additional Info

  - > **Order Number in Notes found  
    > ** Use it when contacting vendor or checking vendor portal.

  - > **No Order Number found

  - > **No Invoice  
    > ** Item likely not shipped.

  - > **Invoice Present  
    > ** Vendor has shipped.

  - > **Partial Invoice  
    > ** Partial shipment has occurred.

**Invoice is generated only after vendor ships.

  - > No Invoice = Not shipped

  - > Invoice Present = Shipped

  - > Partial Invoice = Partial shipment

  - > If backorder exists, state that clearly

  - > If applicable, provide:
    
      - > ETA to warehouse

**Always avoid**

  - > Saying “it will arrive by \[date\]” when the date is only a warehouse ETA

  - > Merging split-order timelines into one unclear statement

### **Phase 6: Documentation**

## **6.1 Coming In House**

### **Meaning**

Inventory is expected to arrive at our warehouse within an expected timeframe.

### **When Used**

Used when the item is available with the vendor and has been ordered to ship to our warehouse before final customer delivery.

### **Action Required**

1.  > Start with Backoffice ETA check.

2.  > Validate whether the vendor actually shipped.

3.  > Coordinate with the warehouse if ETA is near or has passed.

4.  > If unclear, follow up with the vendor for shipping confirmation.

5.  > Inform customer with warehouse ETA plus **1-week delivery buffer**.

### **Detailed Procedure**

**Phase 1: Backoffice**

  - > Open order

  - > Verify status

  - > Check ETA

  - > Compare Qty Ordered vs Qty Shipped

**Phase 2: Validate ETA**

  - > If ETA available: use it as reference

  - > If ETA missing or past: check warehouse first if receipt is possible, then vendor order

**Phase 3: Vendor Order Check**

  - > Review notes and invoice

  - > Apply invoice logic

  - > Check vendor website if available

**Phase 4: Contact Vendor**

  - > Ask for shipping confirmation and ETA to warehouse

**Phase 5: Customer Update**

  - > Provide ETA to warehouse plus one-week delivery estimate afterward

**Phase 6: Follow-Up**

  - > If ETA available and not yet reached: monitor

  - > If ETA crossed by 1–2 days: proactively monitor warehouse and follow up if needed

  - > If ETA missed: immediate vendor follow-up

  - > While waiting:
    
      - > Email: 2 times/week
    
      - > Call: 2–3 times/week

### **Example**

The customer asks for an update. BO shows ETA on March 25. No warehouse receipt yet.  
You tell the customer the item is expected at our warehouse by March 25 and should ship out within about one week after receipt.

  - > ETA has passed

  - > Order is urgent or time-sensitive

  - > Packing List Printed is unresolved after required warehouse follow-up limit

## **8. DO’s and DON’Ts**

### **DO**

  - > Always check Backoffice ETA first

  - > Cross-verify with vendor or carrier

  - > Compare Qty Ordered vs Qty Shipped every time

  - > Separate split orders into parts

  - > Be proactive with delays

  - > Set clear expectations

  - > Document every action

### **DON’T**

  - > Share ETA without checking system

  - > Rely only on status name

  - > Rely only on system if details are unclear

  - > Ignore partial shipments

  - > Delay vendor follow-ups

  - > Present warehouse ETA as final customer delivery date

  - > Combine shipped, pending, and BO items into one vague update

## **9. Notes, Cautions, and Always-Avoid Traps**

### **Notes**

  - > Invoice presence is a key validation point.

  - > Partial invoice means partial shipment.

  - > Standard shipping does not automatically include free split shipment.

  - > Internal approval may be needed before offering split shipment.

  - > In-house delivery estimate requires warehouse buffer.

### **Cautions**

  - > “Coming In House” does not mean ready to deliver.

  - > “Placed for Dropship” does not always mean shipped.

  - > “Packing List Printed” does not mean tracking is guaranteed immediately.

  - > “Shipped Orders – Paid” does not mean delivered.

### **Always-Avoid Traps**

  - > Never skip line-level review.

  - > Never assume entire order is shipped because one tracking number exists.

  - > Never assume BO ETA is the final customer delivery date for in-house orders.

  - > Never offer split shipment without cost/approval check.

  - > Never wait passively once ETA is missed.

# **D. Quick Rule Sheet**

## **Ultra-Compressed “If X → Do Y”**

### **Universal**

  - > **If customer asks for update →** Open BO first.

  - > **If checking any order →** Compare Qty Ordered vs Qty Shipped.

  - > **If status is unclear →** Determine whether it is in-house, dropship, partial, split, or BO.

  - > **If ETA is missing or passed →** Validate before speaking.

  - > **If vendor invoice is missing →** Treat as not shipped unless confirmed otherwise.

  - > **If invoice exists →** Treat as shipped/partially shipped depending on details.

  - > **If order is split →** Handle each part separately.

  - > **If order is partial →** Never speak as if whole order shipped.

### **In-House**

  - > **If Coming In House →** BO ETA = ETA to warehouse, not customer.

  - > **If giving in-house ETA to customer →** Add about 1 week after warehouse receipt.

  - > **If in-house ETA is near/passed →** Check warehouse, then vendor.

### **Backorder**

  - > **If status includes BO →** Vendor BO ETA is priority.
