**<span class="underline">QUOTE FOLLOW UP SOP</span>**

# <span class="underline">PURPOSE</span>

Ensure every sales inquiry is worked systematically, quoted accurately, and followed up consistently in order to maximize quote-to-order conversion while maintaining professional customer communication.

# <span class="underline">SCOPE</span>

This SOP applies to all inbound sales inquiries including:

  - > In-stock products

  - > Non-stock products

  - > Vendor-dependent quotes

  - > Custom products

  - > Special order items

  - > Bulk/quantity pricing inquiries

Mandatory for all Sales Representatives handling quotes and follow-ups.

# <span class="underline">ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES</span>

## <span class="underline">Sales Representative</span>

Own the quote from inquiry → quote → follow-up → closure → all aspects

**<span class="underline">Responsibilities:</span>**

  - > Work every assigned quote same business day

  - > Verify pricing, availability, lead time

  - > Confirm customer billing & shipping details

  - > Contact vendor when required

  - > Provide accurate quotes

  - > Follow structured follow-up schedule

  - > Maintain correct quote status in BO

  - > Document all actions in notes

  - > Copy-paste email text into notes (NO screenshots)

  - > Escalate delays or exceptions to manager

  - > Obtain acknowledgment for custom / special order items

  - > Confirm customer receipt of quote when possible**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

# <span class="underline">QUOTE LIFECYCLE OVERVIEW</span>

**<span class="underline">Every quote must move through one of the following statuses:</span>**

1.  > Pending Quote – Waiting for Customer

2.  > Pending Quote – Submitted to Vendor

3.  > Quote Created

4.  > Quote Sent to Customer

5.  > Quote Sent to Customer – 1st Follow-Up

6.  > Quote Sent to Customer – 2nd Follow-Up

7.  > Quote Sent to Customer – 3rd Follow-Up

8.  > Quote Sent to Customer – Final

9.  > Cancelled Quote**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

# <span class="underline">A. DETAILED WRITTEN SOP</span>

# <span class="underline">1. Quote Intake & Initial Contact</span>

### <span class="underline">Timing Requirement</span>

All quotes must be worked on the same business day received.

### <span class="underline">Actions</span>

1.  > Review customer request in the customer comment section if any special requirements has been mentioned by the customer when requesting a quote

2.  > **<span class="underline">Verify information:  
    > </span>**
    
      - > Product SKU
    
      - > Quantity
    
      - > Shipping address
    
      - > Customer contact details and Purchase timeline**<span class="underline">  
        > </span>**

3.  > Attempt initial contact within 1–2 hours**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

### <span class="underline">If customer answers</span>

  - > Confirm details

  - > Prepare quote

  - > Send quote immediately if possible**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

### <span class="underline">If customer does not answer</span>

  - > Send quote same day

  - > Begin follow-up process**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

# <span class="underline">2. Quote Preparation Requirements</span>

**<span class="underline">Every quote must include:</span>**

  - > Item pricing

  - > Availability

  - > Quantity

  - > Shipping charges

  - > Lead time

  - > Billing address

  - > Shipping address

  - > Quote validity

  - > Disclosure if item is custom/special order**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

### <span class="underline">Quote Validity</span>

Standard validity: 30 days (After 30 days a new quote must be generated)

The system displays 10 days to create urgency.

If customer asks:

Agent may say:

> “Since this is your first order / since you’ve been ordering with us regularly, I can hold this quote for up to 30 days.”

# <span class="underline">3. Quote Workflows by Item Type</span>

# <span class="underline">3.1 In-Stock Items</span>

### <span class="underline">Step 1</span>

Prepare a quote immediately.

### <span class="underline">Step 2</span>

Attempt phone contact + personalized email.

### <span class="underline">If customer responds</span>

Follow Quote Delivery Procedure

### <span class="underline">If no response</span>

Attempt contact daily for 3 business days

**<span class="underline">Methods:</span>**

  - > Phone call

  - > Email**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

After 3 attempts:

Move through the Follow up Stages (First, Second, Third, and ending in Final)

# <span class="underline">3.2 Non-Stock Items (Vendor Contact Required)</span>

**<span class="underline">Used when pricing or availability must be obtained from vendor.</span>**

### <span class="underline">Step 1</span>

Contact the vendor immediately.

### <span class="underline">Step 2</span>

Update status:

Pending Quote – Submitted to Vendor

### <span class="underline">Step 3</span>

Send a vendor request email.

### <span class="underline">Step 4</span>

Follow up with vendor every:

1–2 business days

Minimum attempts:

3–4 follow-ups

### <span class="underline">If vendor responds</span>

Prepare the quote and send it to the customer.

### <span class="underline">If vendor does not respond</span>

After required attempts:

Report to Supervisor

# <span class="underline">3.3 Missing Customer Information</span>

Used when quotes cannot be prepared.

Common missing items:

  - > Quantity

  - > Product specification

  - > Shipping address

  - > Customer clarification**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

### <span class="underline">Actions</span>

1.  > Contact customer immediately

2.  > Send request email

3.  > Update status:

Pending Quote – Waiting for Customer

### <span class="underline">Follow-up schedule</span>

Follow up every:

24–48 hours

**<span class="underline">Minimum attempts:</span>**

3–4

### <span class="underline">If still no response</span>

Cancel quote.

# <span class="underline">3.4 Custom / Special Order Items</span>

**<span class="underline">These require extra verification.</span>**

### <span class="underline">Mandatory Steps</span>

1.  > Confirm specifications with customer

2.  > Contact vendor for pricing & lead time

3.  > Multiply vendor cost by x1.5 to find unit price

4.  > Confirm shipping costs

5.  > Add lead time buffer

6.  > Add special disclaimer

> **<span class="underline">  
> </span>**

### <span class="underline">Mandatory Disclosure</span>

Custom & Special Orders are:

NON-CANCELABLE AND NON-RETURNABLE

### <span class="underline">Important Rules</span>

**<span class="underline">DO NOT SEND QUOTE UNTIL:</span>**

  - > Price confirmed

  - > Lead time confirmed

  - > Availability confirmed

  - > Do not provide a discount**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

# <span class="underline">4. Quote Delivery Procedure</span>

**<span class="underline">When quote is ready:</span>**

### <span class="underline">Step 1</span>

Send Quote Invoice to customer.

  - > Attach quote to email

  - > If quote is high value over 5,000, special order, or custom you must get a signed quote

<span class="underline">Step 2</span>

**<span class="underline">Call the Customer</span>**

### <span class="underline">Step 3</span>

**<span class="underline">Confirm:</span>**

  - > Quote received

  - > Purchase timeline

  - > Customer details accuracy**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

### <span class="underline">Step 4</span>

Update BO status:

Quote Sent to Customer

# <span class="underline">5. Follow-Up Schedule</span>

**<span class="underline">Before each follow-up:</span>**

Sales rep must:

  - > Review previous notes

  - > Verify availability again

  - > Confirm vendor status if applicable**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

### <span class="underline">Follow-Up Timeline</span>

| Follow Up | Timeline | Status          |
| --------- | -------- | --------------- |
| Initial   | Day 0    | Quote Sent      |
| 1st       | Day 3    | 1st Follow-Up   |
| 2nd       | Day 5    | 2nd Follow-Up   |
| 3rd       | Day 9    | 3rd Follow-Up   |
| Final     | Day 15   | Final Follow-Up |

# <span class="underline">6. Quote Cancellation</span>

Quotes may be cancelled if:

  - > Customer purchased elsewhere

  - > Customer requested cancellation

  - > Vendor cannot provide product

  - > Project cancelled

  - > Customer unresponsive**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

  - > Lead time too high**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

### <span class="underline">Required Action</span>

1.  > Document reason

2.  > Update BO status

3.  > Send closing email

  - > Offer alternatives when possible

  - > Maintain accurate notes

  - > Copy/paste email communications into notes

# <span class="underline">D. QUICK RULE SHEET (CHEAT SHEET)</span>

### <span class="underline">Rules:</span>

If inquiry received → Work same business day

If item in stock → Send quote same day

If vendor info required → Contact vendor immediately

If customer info missing → Move to Waiting for Customer

If custom item → Confirm specs before quoting

If no customer response → Follow structured follow-ups

### <span class="underline">Follow-Up Timing</span>

Day 0 → Quote sent  
Day 3 → 1st Follow-Up  
Day 5 → 2nd Follow-Up  
Day 9 → 3rd Follow-Up  
Day 15 → Final Follow-Up

### <span class="underline">Cancellation Rule</span>

Cancel when:

  - > Customer unresponsive

  - > Customer declines

  - > Project cancelled**<span class="underline">  
    > </span>**

### <span class="underline">Always Do</span>

✔ Document every action  
✔ Copy-paste notes (no screenshots)  
✔ Verify lead time  
✔ Confirm availability  
✔ Save status changes

### <span class="underline">Never Do</span>

✖ Send quote without confirmed price  
✖ Send custom quote without lead time  
✖ Skip follow-ups  
✖ Leave status outdated  
✖ Forget to save status
