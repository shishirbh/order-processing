**1.2 Order Types**

**1.2.1 In-House Orders**

  - > Vendor ships items to DK Hardware, and DK reships to the customer.

  - > Use In-House When:

<!-- end list -->

  - > Customer is closer to DK than the vendor.

  - > Consolidation is required.

  - > Lengthy/oversized items are involved (safer handling).

  - > Dropship cost will exceed DK’s shipping cost.

  - > DK must inspect or combine multiple items.

**Example:**

Customer is in California, DK warehouse is closer than the vendor → In-house.

**1.2.2 Dropship Orders (Email Only)**

  - > Vendor ships directly to the customer using DK’s FedEx account.

  - > Use Dropship When:

  - > Order is 1–2 Business Days urgent.

  - > Customer is closer to the vendor than DK.

  - > Shipping speed from DK cannot meet customer delivery expectations.

  - > Oversized items → customer is a nearby vendor (faster & cheaper).

**1.2.3 Avoid Dropship When:**

  - > Multiple items must ship together.

  - > DK shipping is cheaper than vendor-to-customer.

  - > Customer requires DK packaging or inspection.

  - > High risk of delivery issues at customer locations.

**SECTION 2 — PROCESSING ORDERS**

# **2.1 PROCESSING A VENDOR ORDER — IN-HOUSE**

2.1.1 Step 1 — Confirm item availability (vendor rarely refuses).  
2.1.2 Step 2 — Create In-House Vendor Order in DK system.  
2.1.3 Step 3 — Email vendor using the In-House Template.  
2.1.4 Step 4 — Vendor processes (usually on Wednesday unless early requested).  
2.1.5 Step 5 — Receive shipment at DK.  
2.1.6 Step 6 — DK ships to customers as per DK shipping rules.

### **2.1.7 In-House Email Template**

Hello,

Please process this order, if possible for delivery tomorrow.

Kindly let us know if there are any changes.

Regards,

Purchasing Team

(Your Name)

**2.1.8 Example:  
**Customer in Nevada → DK warehouse closer → In-house.

# **2.2 PROCESSING A VENDOR ORDER — DROPSHIP**

2.2.1 Step 1 — Confirm the customer’s address.  
2.2.2 Step 2 — Create Dropship Vendor Order in the DK system.  
2.2.3 Step 3 — Email vendor with:

  - > Shipping address

  - > DK FedEx account number

  - > PO (if required)

2.2.4 Step 4 — Vendor acknowledges.  
2.2.5 Step 5 — Vendor ships directly to customer.

### **2.2.6 Dropship Email Template**

Hello,

Please ship this order directly to our customer.

SHIP VIA FEDEX USING OUR ACCOUNT.

Kindly let us know if there are any changes.

Regards,

Purchasing Team

(Your Name)

**2.2.7 Example:  
** Customer in Pennsylvania wants 1-day delivery → Dropship via FedEx.
