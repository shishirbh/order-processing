**SECTION 2 - ITEM CATEGORIES AND SHIPPING METHODS**

1.  > **ITEM CATEGORIES**

**1.1 Standard Item (= 95" or below) -** Processed in Miami Main unless special rules apply. These are NOT long items.

> **Routing:**

  - > Miami Main (default)

  - > Dropship if customer near WH

  - > Dropship if urgent

  - > Miami not used for taxes (tax-exempt states)

  - > Examples will appear under the Miami Main section.

**1.2 Long Item (\>95") -** Mandatory routing to Miami Long unless Out-of-Stock. Automatically treated as long items.

> Routing:

  - > Miami Long

  - > If Miami Long OOS → LAX or nearest WH

  - > Dropship preferred in many cases

  - > Freight must be validated

  - > Many cannot be cut (customer instructions)

  - > Examples in Part B.

**1.3 Extra-long items-** (example 107”, 120”, 144”, 172”, 240”) Each length has:

  - > Special freight rules (See Tables 7, 8 and 9)

  - > Special carrier restrictions

  - > Special routing logic

  - > Higher risk of damage

  - > Cutting constraints

Triggers:

  - > Oversize category

  - > Higher shipping cost

  - > More damage risk

  - > Dropship strongly recommended if customers near regional WH.

**1.4 Freight Item** - Any item requiring LTL freight shipment. Examples:

  - > Very long items

  - > Heavy base shoes

  - > Large glass components

  - > Multi-piece long bundles

  - > Large hardware packages

These are extremely long items. Handling rules:

  - > Miami cannot always receive them safely

  - > Freight inbound + outbound is extremely high

  - > Damage risk increases

  - > Must check if customer accepts cutting

  - > Dropship often cheaper and faster

  - > Freight can exceed margin

  - > These will be fully detailed in Part B and Part C.

**SECTION 4 - EXECUTION OF CUSTOMER AND VENDOR ORDERS BASED ON CRL WAREHOUSES**

1.  > **MAIMI MAIN SOP (Standard Items ≤95”):**

Miami Main handles ALL standard-sized CRL items that are 95” or below. This includes:

  - > Hinges

  - > Handles

  - > Brackets

  - > Shower hardware

  - > Clamps

  - > Screws

  - > Small extrusions

  - > Patch fittings

  - > Any item listed under “Stock: MIA” on CRL website

  - > Anything NOT flagged as extra-long

**1.1 When Miami Main Must be Used:**

Miami Main MUST be used when:

  - > Item is ≤95"

  - > Miami has stock

  - > Customer is NOT near another WH that offers faster dropship

  - > Item is small/moderate size (hardware)

  - > Order is NOT urgent and ETA is flexible

  - > The order is for:

<!-- end list -->

  - > Zoro (required)

  - > Tax-exempt states

  - > Low-margin orders

<!-- end list -->

  - > Customer location does not significantly benefit from dropship

  - > Marketplace is NOT pushing for quicker fulfillment

**1.2 Step-by-Step Miami Main Workflow:**

1.2.1 STEP 1 — Verify item length

Check:

  - > CRL website

  - > Product specifications

  - > Packaging length

  - > If ≤95” → item qualifies

1.2.2 STEP 2 — Check Miami Main stock

  - > If in stock → proceed Miami pickup.

  - > If Out-of-Stock → escalate to CRL Other Warehouses logic.

1.2.3 STEP 3 — Confirm customer geography

If customer lives in:

  - > CA

  - > WA

  - > OR

  - > NV

  - > AZ

Miami Main may not be optimal — check regional WH for dropship.

1.2.4 STEP 4 — Evaluate urgency

  - > If urgent → consider dropship.

1.2.5 STEP 5 — Margin check

  - > If margin \< $10 → Miami pickup preferred (dropship avoided).

1.2.6 STEP 6 — Create Vendor Order (VO)

  - > Create VO with Warehouse: MIAMI MAIN (01)

  - > Add SHIP/CANCEL instructions

  - > Use UPS Ground

**1.3 SPECIAL CASES in Miami Main:**

1.3.1 CASE A — Customer near another WH (e.g., NY)

If ship-to NY and BOS has stock → dropship BOS may be faster.

1.3.2 CASE B — Amazon SLA

Miami may not meet delivery speed → dropship required.

1.3.3 CASE C — Heavy item \<95”

Dropship if near regional WH (to avoid heavy inbound).

1.3.4 CASE D — Mixed order (long + small)

Small item can:

  - > go with long-item dropship

  - > OR be brought in-house separately.

2.  > **MAIMI LONG SOP (LONG ITEMS \>95”)**

Miami Long handles ALL items longer than 95 inches. CRL defines long items as any SKU with a physical length beyond 95”. Many mistakes happen in this category therefore be extra careful while processing such orders.

Items Handled by Miami Long include (examples):

  - > Any extrusion \>95”

  - > Thresholds

  - > Shower door channels

  - > Aluminum bars

  - > Base shoes

  - > PVC covers

  - > 120” pieces

  - > 144” pieces

  - > 172” pieces

  - > 240” pieces

  - > Uncut long items

  - > Rolls (special handling)

**2.1 When Miami Long MUST be used:**

  - > Item \>95”

  - > Customer located close to Florida

  - > Miami Long shows stock

  - > Order is NOT urgent

  - > Customer accepts standard ground freight

  - > Cutting required (Miami handles cutting reliably)

**2.2 When Miami Long SHOULD NOT be used:**

  - > When item exceeds 107” and customer is far from Miami

  - > When freight \> profit

  - > When customer clearly benefits from regional WH

  - > When item is extremely long (144"/172"/240") and Miami lacks handling equipment

  - > When customer says “DO NOT CUT” but Miami only has cut-length variants

  - > When Miami is OOS and LAX has stock (dropship better)

**2.3 Step-by-Step Miami Long Workflow**

2.3.1 STEP 1 — Verify actual length

  - > Use CRL specs, not DKH website.

2.3.2 STEP 2 — Check Miami Long stock

  - > IF IN STOCK → proceed Miami Long.

  - > IF Out-of-Stock →

<!-- end list -->

  - > Check LAX

  - > Check regional WH

  - > Check alternate vendors

  - > Evaluate freight

2.3.3 STEP 3 — Check customer location

  - > If customer in:

<!-- end list -->

  - > CA / NV / WA / OR → LAX faster

  - > Midwest → Chicago faster

  - > Northeast → BOS/NY faster

<!-- end list -->

  - > Dropship recommended.

2.3.4 STEP 4 — Cutting Instructions

  - > If CO notes:

<!-- end list -->

  - > “Do not cut”

<!-- end list -->

  - > Required:

<!-- end list -->

  - > Dropship from WH carrying full uncut length

  - > Never bring to Miami if Miami cuts by default

2.3.5 STEP 5 — Freight Validation

  - > Long items often exceed parcel limits.

  - > Before routing:

<!-- end list -->

  - > Validate CRL freight table (in SECTION 2 Part 2)

  - > Evaluate margins

  - > Evaluate customer distance

  - > Evaluate shipping method

2.3.6 STEP 6 — Create VO

  - > Warehouse: MIAMI LONG

  - > Shipping: UPS Ground or LTL if required

  - > Add full length notes

  - > Add cutting instructions clearly

**2.4 SPECIAL CASES in Miami Long**

2.4.1 CASE A — Miami Long OOS but LAX has stock \>95”

Dropship from LAX is preferred unless:

  - > Customer is in tax-exempt state

  - > Customer explicitly wants in-house

  - > Zoro order

2.4.2 CASE B — 144”, 172”, 240” lengths

Dropship is almost always required.

2.4.3 CASE C — Customer requires cutting

Miami Long best handles cutting instructions.

2.4.4 CASE D — Bulk long-item orders (\>8 pieces)

Freight inbound to Miami can be cheaper than multiple dropships.

**SECTION 11 - SPECIAL & CORNER CASE RULES**

This section addresses situations where normal logic breaks

1.  > **Corner Case: FULL-LENGTH ONLY Needed**

If customer says:

  - > “DO NOT CUT”

  - > “I need full 144 inches”

  - > “Send uncut extrusion”

Then:

✔ Must dropship from WH that has full uncut stock

✔ NEVER send to Miami if Miami stores cut stock

✔ Miami Long cuts 95% of long items

Example:

  - > Item 144”

  - > Miami stock: 144” but cuts by default

  - > BOS: 144” uncut

→ Dropship BOS.

2.  > **Corner Case: Freight-Only Items**

Items like:

  - > Rolls

  - > Windshield panels

  - > Big glass channels

Rules:

✔ Must be ordered from Miami only

✔ CRL Others cannot handle these safely

✔ Freight cost higher → Miami pickup avoids inbound cost

Example:

Roll item → Always Miami Long.

3.  > **Corner Case: Small Item in Long Vendor Order**

If customer orders:

  - > 1 long item (120”) and

  - > 1 small item (clamp)

Options:

  - > If customer near WH:

→ Dropship BOTH

  - > If customer far:

→ Bring long item in-house, small item in-house

  - > If small item available regionally but long item only in Miami:

→ Split orders:

  - > Long → Miami

  - > Small → Dropship

<!-- end list -->

4.  > **Corner Case: Multi-Warehouse Split Stock**

If stock is scattered:

  - > Miami: 0

  - > LAX: 1

  - > ATL: 2

  - > CHI: 1

Rule:

  - > “Fulfill from a single warehouse whenever possible.”

So:

  - > Choose ATL (2 pcs)

  - > Move LAX + CHI pieces to ATL

  - > OR dropship from ATL

This reduces:

  - > Freight

  - > Complexity

  - > Shipment delays

<!-- end list -->

5.  > **Corner Case: Large Quantity Orders**

10+ pieces long items behave differently.

If customer in CA:

  - > Miami → inbound freight $400

  - > LAX → freight $80

Dropship saves $320.

If customer in FL:

  - > Reverse logic: Miami cheaper.

<!-- end list -->

6.  > **Corner Case: Hazardous Materials**

Hazmat examples:

  - > Primers

  - > Adhesives

  - > Sealants

Rules:

✔ Dropship allowed if WH can ship hazmat

✔ If hazmat amount is low

→ Better in-house

✔ If WH has only 1 unit left

→ Do NOT dropship (risk of cancellation)

Example : DKH-2436323 Hazmat item L5800QT, only 1 unit left

→ RULE: Do NOT dropship; order in-house

7.  > **Corner Case: Tax-Exempt State & Urgent Order**

If customer in Oregon AND Amazon SLA is urgent:

Standard rule:

  - > No dropship to tax-free states.

Exception rule:

Dropship allowed IF:

  - > It is expedited (1-day/2-day)

  - > Margin supports tax variation

  - > Supervisor approves

<!-- end list -->

8.  > **Corner Case: CRL Transfer Days**

CRL has an important transfer rule:

  - > If Miami order is processed before Wednesday → LAX → MIA transfer arrives by next Wednesday

  - > If processed after Wednesday → transfer takes 2 weeks

This affects long items significantly.

9.  > **Corner Case: Mixed Vendor History**

If item shows:

  - > Past fulfilled by Strybuc

  - > Recently fulfilled by CRL

→ Use CRL unless Strybuc-only stock exists
